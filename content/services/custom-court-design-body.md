## Court Builder

Court Design works best when the same team understands both planning and construction. Hatz Court Builders connects design decisions to real build conditions — soil, grade, access, climate, and long-term maintenance.

We work with homeowners, schools, HOAs, parks, clubs, and commercial clients who want a clear plan before construction begins.

A professional court builder can help answer questions like:

- What court size fits this property?
- How should the court be oriented for sun, wind, and access?
- Which surface system is the best match?
- How should multi-sport striping be organized?
- Where should fencing, lighting, and equipment go?
- Can the design leave room for future expansion?

Every design starts with the site and the way the court will actually be used.

### Site Planning And Layout

Court placement affects drainage, access, privacy, noise, and play quality. We review available space, slope, surrounding features, and safety zones before locking in a layout.

### Surface And Striping Plans

Surface choice and striping hierarchy are major design decisions. Multi-sport courts need clear line colors and equipment plans so each sport remains easy to play.

### Lighting, Fencing, And Equipment

Lighting, fencing, nets, hoops, goals, and seating should be designed with the court — not added as an afterthought. Good placement improves safety, usability, and the finished look.

## Common Problems With Poor Court Design

Common issues include courts that face the wrong direction, lines that are hard to read, equipment in the wrong place, drainage that fails after rain, and layouts that leave too little room for safe play.
