## Court Builder

Commercial projects need a court builder who understands shared-use facilities. Peak traffic, maintenance access, spectator flow, lighting, fencing, and safety zones all matter.

Hatz Court Builders works with facility managers, schools, municipalities, HOA boards, clubs, and commercial property owners to plan courts that match the way the site will operate day to day.

A professional court builder can help answer questions like:

- How much daily traffic will the court see?
- Which surface system holds up best for commercial use?
- Should this be a single court or a multi-court facility?
- What fencing, lighting, and access are required?
- Can branding, logos, or custom colors be included?
- How will maintenance and future expansion be handled?

Site conditions, drainage, and intended programming all influence the final design and construction process.

## Commercial Court Construction

Commercial Court Construction starts with understanding who will use the court and how often. A school PE court, a park recreation court, and a private club facility each have different needs.

Our Commercial Court Construction services include:

- School and university athletic courts
- Park and municipal recreation courts
- HOA and apartment community courts
- Club and training facility courts
- Resort and hospitality courts
- Church and community courts
- Multi-court commercial recreation facilities

The goal is a court system that looks professional, drains correctly, and stands up to frequent play.

### Schools And Parks

Schools and parks need durable courts that support classes, leagues, open play, and community programs. Layouts may include basketball, pickleball, tennis, volleyball, futsal, or multi-sport striping.

### HOAs And Apartment Communities

Shared residential communities often want recreation amenities that appeal to a wide range of residents. Commercial-grade construction helps those courts hold up under regular use.

### Clubs, Resorts, And Athletic Facilities

Clubs and resorts may need branded courts, lighting for evening play, fencing, seating, and premium surface systems that match the property experience.

Common issues include surfaces that wear too quickly, poor drainage under heavy use, inadequate safety space, weak fencing or lighting plans, confusing multi-sport striping, and courts that are hard to maintain.
