## Court Builder

A skilled court builder looks at the whole project, not just the final surface.

Before a basketball court is built, the site needs to be reviewed. The court location, slope, base, drainage, sun exposure, access, and surrounding space all affect the finished result.

Hatz Court Builders works with homeowners, schools, HOAs, parks, gyms, churches, recreation centers, and commercial property owners. We help clients plan courts that match the way people will actually use the space.

A court builder can help answer questions like:

- Should this be a half court or full court?
- How much safety space is needed around the court?
- What surface works best for basketball?
- Can the court include pickleball or volleyball lines?
- Where should the hoop system be placed?
- Will the court drain correctly?
- Can the court be expanded later?

Basketball courts take more impact than many other court types. Players run, jump, pivot, stop fast, and change direction over and over. That is why the court system needs to be built with strength, traction, and long-term use in mind.

### Backyard Basketball Courts

Backyard Basketball Court Construction is a popular choice for homeowners who want a private place to play, practice, and spend time outside.

A backyard court can be simple or fully customized. Some homeowners want a clean half court with one hoop. Others want a full court, multi-use court, custom colors, lighting, fencing, or added pickleball lines.

When planning a backyard basketball court, we look at available yard space, property slope, drainage, access for construction, hoop placement, safety space, surface options, nearby landscaping, noise and privacy, and future court use.

The court should feel like it belongs on the property. It should be easy to access, comfortable to use, and built in a location that makes sense for the home.

### Half-Court Basketball Construction

Half-court basketball construction is a great option when space is limited or when the main goal is casual play, shooting practice, or one-on-one games.

A half court can fit many residential yards, school areas, church properties, apartment communities, and HOA recreation spaces. Even though a half court is smaller than a full court, it still needs proper construction. The base, surface, slope, hoop placement, and safety area all matter.

### Full Basketball Court Construction

Full Basketball Court Construction is ideal for schools, parks, gyms, training facilities, clubs, and larger residential or commercial properties.

A full court gives players the space needed for full games, drills, conditioning, team practices, and organized play. Full court projects may include two hoop systems, full basketball striping, safety zones, lighting, fencing, seating areas, walkways, multi-court layouts, custom colors, and surface upgrades.
