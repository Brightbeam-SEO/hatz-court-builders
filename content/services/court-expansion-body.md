## Court Builder

Court Expansion is not just pouring more surface next to an old court. The existing base, slope, drainage, striping, and equipment all need to work with the new area.

Hatz Court Builders reviews the current facility before recommending expansion, resurfacing, conversion, or a full rebuild when the existing court is past its useful life.

A professional court builder can help answer questions like:

- Can the current court support an expansion?
- Is there enough room for another court or a larger layout?
- Should we add a new sport or expand the existing one?
- Do fencing and lighting need to be updated?
- Will drainage still work after the expansion?
- Is restriping enough, or do we need new construction?

Every expansion project depends on site access, available space, and the condition of what is already there.

## Court Expansion

Court Expansion starts with a clear goal. Some clients need more pickleball capacity. Others want to add basketball, create a multi-sport layout, or enlarge a school or HOA recreation area.

Our Court Expansion services include:

- Adding pickleball courts to existing facilities
- Expanding basketball half courts into larger courts
- Tennis court additions and tennis-to-pickleball conversions
- Multi-court expansions for schools and parks
- HOA and apartment recreation upgrades
- Commercial facility court additions
- Resurfacing and restriping as part of expansion projects

The goal is more usable playing space without creating drainage, access, or layout problems.

### Residential Court Expansion

Homeowners may expand a backyard half court, add pickleball lines, or create a larger multi-use recreation area beside an existing court. Even residential expansions need proper grading and surface planning.

### School And Park Expansion

Schools and parks often expand when programs grow or community demand increases. Added courts, multi-sport striping, lighting, and fencing can help a facility serve more players.

### HOA And Commercial Expansion

HOAs, clubs, resorts, and commercial properties expand recreation amenities to reduce wait times, support more residents or guests, and keep facilities competitive.

## Common Problems With Poor Court Expansion

Common issues include mismatched surfaces, drainage problems at the joint between old and new areas, cramped safety space, fencing that no longer fits, and expansions that look patched instead of planned.
