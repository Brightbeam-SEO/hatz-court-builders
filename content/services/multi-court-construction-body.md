## Court Builder

A multi-court project needs a builder who understands how different sports use the same surface. Line colors, net systems, hoop placement, runoff space, and traffic patterns all have to work together.

Hatz Court Builders works with residential and commercial clients to create multi-court systems that stay clear, durable, and easy to use.

A professional court builder can help answer questions like:

- Which sports should share the same court?
- How should striping colors be organized?
- Will equipment conflict between sports?
- How much safety space is needed for each activity?
- Should this be one shared court or a multi-court facility?
- Can an existing court be converted into a multi-sport layout?

Soil conditions, grading, weather exposure, and intended use all influence the final design and construction process.

## Multi-Court Construction

Multi-Court Construction starts with prioritizing the sports that matter most. Some clients want pickleball as the primary sport with basketball as a secondary option. Others need school or park layouts that support several activities throughout the day.

Popular multi-court combinations include:

- Pickleball and basketball
- Tennis and pickleball
- Basketball and volleyball
- Futsal and basketball
- Pickleball, basketball, and tennis practice
- Multi-sport recreation complexes

Our Multi-Court Construction services include backyard multi-sport courts, HOA recreation courts, school athletic courts, park multi-use courts, club training courts, and commercial multi-court facilities.

### Backyard Multi-Sport Courts

Homeowners often choose Multi-Court Construction when yard space is limited but the family wants more than one way to play. A dual-sport backyard court can support pickleball and basketball, or another combination that fits the property.

### School And Park Multi-Court Facilities

Schools and parks benefit from courts that serve different programs, age groups, and peak-use times. Multi-court layouts can support PE classes, after-school play, community recreation, and organized sports with one durable surface system.

### HOA And Commercial Multi-Court Projects

HOAs, apartment communities, clubs, and commercial facilities often need recreation amenities that appeal to a wide range of residents and guests. A multi-court area can deliver more value than a single-sport court in the same footprint.

## Common Problems With Poor Multi-Court Construction

Common issues include confusing line colors, equipment conflicts, tight runoff space, poor drainage, surfaces that do not suit all intended sports, and layouts that favor one activity while making others awkward to play.
