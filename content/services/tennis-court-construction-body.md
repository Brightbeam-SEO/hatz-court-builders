A tennis court is a long-term investment in recreation, training, property value, and outdoor use. For homeowners, it can turn an open space into a private place to play. For schools, clubs, parks, and commercial properties, it can create a dependable athletic area that serves players for years.

But a tennis court needs more than a large paved surface and painted lines. The court must have the right base, slope, drainage, surfacing, fencing, net system, court orientation, and safety space. If the court is not planned correctly, it can crack, hold water, fade too quickly, or feel uneven during play.

## Court Builder

A good court builder does more than install a surface. The full court system has to work together.

Before tennis court construction begins, the property needs to be reviewed carefully. The court location, grade, access, drainage, sun direction, nearby structures, and future use all affect the final build.

Hatz Court Builders works with homeowners, schools, HOAs, parks, private clubs, recreation centers, resorts, and commercial property owners.

A court builder can help answer questions like:

- Where should the tennis court be placed?
- How much space is needed around the court?
- What surface system is best for tennis?
- Should the court include pickleball lines?
- Will fencing or lighting be needed?
- Can an old court be resurfaced or expanded?
- How should drainage be handled?
